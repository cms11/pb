Guiding Principles / Summary

What are the Guiding Principles for social sustainability?

The Guiding Principles for social sustainability are a set of principles that provide guidance on how to achieve sustainable development in a way that benefits all members of society. The principles were developed by the United Nations Global Compact and are based on internationally recognized human rights standards.

What are the main principles of the Guiding Principles for social sustainability?

The main principles of the Guiding Principles for social sustainability are: the promotion of human rights, the promotion of labor rights, the promotion of environmental protection, the promotion of anti-corruption measures, and the promotion of sustainable consumption and production patterns.

How can companies use the Guiding Principles for social sustainability to improve their sustainability performance?

Companies can use the Guiding Principles for social sustainability as a framework for implementing sustainable practices and policies in their operations. By aligning their business practices with the principles, companies can demonstrate their commitment to social sustainability and improve their sustainability performance. This can help to enhance the company's reputation and build trust with stakeholders, as well as drive positive change and create long-term value for the company and society.
